  1. Magnetic field lines never cross each other.

  3. They always emerge from the North Pole and terminate at the South Pole.
  4. The direction of the magnetic field is the direction in which the north pole of a compass needle moves inside it.

Essay Essay Questions
30 questions
Q1What does wave-particle duality mean?▾
The wave behaves as a particle and the particle behaves as a wave.
Q2What are the properties of the electromagnetic spectrum?▾
  1. Vibrating electric and magnetic fields that move through space or any medium.
  2. Transverse waves only.
  3. Don't need medium to travel through.
  4. Propagate in space at the same speed (c = 3×10⁸ m/s) while varying in frequency and wavelength; propagate in material media with smaller velocity depending on the medium.
Q3What are the wavelengths of the visible light spectrum?▾
400 nm – 700 nm (approximately)
Q4Why do different objects look different colors?▾
Because the dominant color of light (wavelength) emitted from these sources varies according to the temperature and properties of the object.
Q5What happens to object color as temperature changes?▾
At low temperatures, wavelengths are mainly in the infrared region. As temperature increases, the object begins to glow red. At high temperatures, it appears white.
Q6Why does an enclosed cavity appear black?▾
Because all radiation within the cavity remains trapped due to multiple reflections.
Q7Mention applications of remote sensing.▾
  1. Satellites and airborne which map and photograph the earth's surface.
  2. Military purposes such as night vision systems.
  3. In medicine (tomography – embryology).
  4. In criminology (heat radiated from a person remains even after they leave).
Q8Why did classical physics fail to explain blackbody radiation?▾
Because classical physics considers radiation as electromagnetic waves (wave theory), so intensity should increase with increasing frequency, becoming infinite at high frequencies. But experimental data shows intensity decreases at high frequency.
Q9What is Planck's explanation of blackbody radiation?▾
  1. Radiation is made up of small units (packets) of energy called quanta (photons).
  2. Energy of EM waves is quantized (E = nhf) rather than continuous.
  3. Energy could be gained or lost only in integral multiples.
  4. The atom does not radiate as long as it remains in one energy level.
  5. If atom shifts from high to lower energy level, it emits a photon with energy E = hf.
Q10What are the functions of C.R.T parts?▾
  1. Filament: Heat up the cathode.
  2. Grid: Control the number of electrons in the beam.
  3. Anode: Accelerate the electron beam towards the screen.
  4. Electron gun: Produce a beam of electrons at high speed.
  5. Fluorescent Screen: Emits green light when electrons strike it.
  6. Deflecting system: Sweep the screen point by point (raster).
Q11Effect of increasing frequency of incident light (if > work function) on: (1) KE of released electron, (2) Photocurrent intensity.▾
  1. Kinetic energy of released electron: Increases.
  2. Intensity of photocurrent: Remains constant (unchanged).
Q12Effect of increasing intensity of incident light (if > work function) on: (1) KE of released electron, (2) Photocurrent intensity.▾
  1. Kinetic energy of released electron: Remains constant (unchanged).
  2. Intensity of photocurrent: Increases.
Q13What are the basic properties of photons?▾
  1. Electromagnetic radiation travels as discrete packets of energy (quanta).
  2. Travel in straight lines at speed of light (c = 3×10⁸ m/s).
  3. Energy: E = hf = hc/λ
  4. Momentum: p = hf/c = h/λ
  5. Electrically neutral; not deflected by electric or magnetic fields.
Q14What are the properties of X-rays?▾
  1. They can penetrate media easily.
  2. They can ionize gases.
  3. They diffract in crystals.
  4. They affect sensitive photographic plates.
Q15What is the function of components in Coolidge tube?▾
  1. Filament (cathode): source of electrons, made of tungsten, coiled for high resistance.
  2. Solid copper rod (anode).
  3. Heavy element (Molybdenum) as target.
  4. External high DC voltage source.
  5. Battery supplying high current to heat the cathode filament.
  6. Vacuum tube to avoid collisions with air molecules.
  7. Cooling fins to reduce heating effect.
Q16How to produce X-rays using Coolidge tube?▾
  1. Electrons emitted from the filament experience the potential difference and accelerate towards the target.
  2. When electrons collide with the target element, their kinetic energy converts to X-rays.
Q17What are the kinds of X-ray spectrum?▾
  1. Continuous Spectrum: all wavelengths regardless of target material.
  2. Line Spectrum (Characteristic Radiation): specific wavelengths characteristic of the target material.
Q18If voltage between cathode and anode increases in Coolidge tube, effect on: (1) Continuous radiation, (2) Characteristic radiation.▾
  1. Minimum wavelength of continuous radiation decreases.
  2. Wavelengths of characteristic radiation don't change.
Q19If atomic number of target element increases, effect on: (1) Continuous radiation, (2) Characteristic radiation.▾
  1. Wavelengths of continuous radiation don't change.
  2. Wavelengths of characteristic radiation decrease.
Q20Why are X-rays used to study crystalline structure?▾
X-rays have the ability to diffract as they penetrate materials.
Q21Why are X-rays used to detect defects in metallic structures?▾
X-rays have great penetrating power.
Q22Mention applications of X-rays.▾
  1. Studying the crystalline structure.
  2. Detecting defects in metallic structures.
  3. In medicine: imaging bones and fractures.
Q23What does LASER stand for?▾
Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation.

  1. Monochromaticity.

Q1State two methods to increase the conductivity of semiconductors.▾
  1. Increasing the temperature.
  2. Doping process.
Q2Why at very low temperature, pure silicon crystal is an insulator.▾
Because all bonds in the crystal are unbroken and it has high resistance to current flow (no free electrons). At 0K, Silicon behaves as a perfect insulator.
Q3Compare forward bias and reverse bias effect on thickness of depletion layer.▾
  • Forward bias: the depletion layer is narrow because the battery field is opposite to the internal field.
  • Reverse bias: the depletion layer is wide because the battery field aids the internal field.
Q4Mention two uses of diodes.▾
  • Current rectifier.
  • Switch.
Q5How to use ohmmeter to distinguish between ohmic resistance and PN junction?▾
  • Ohmic resistance: magnitude of current is the same regardless of voltage polarity.
  • PN junction: small resistance in forward bias, large resistance in reverse bias.
Q6What does BJT stand for?▾
Bipolar Junction Transistor.
Q7What is the main use of a transistor?▾
As an amplifier.
Q8What is the basic idea of transistor action as an amplifier?▾
Any small change in the base current I_B will result in a much larger change in the collector current I_C.
Q9Why are most modern electronic devices made from semiconductors?▾
Because they are sensitive to environmental conditions.
